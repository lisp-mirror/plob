Tutorial: Persistent LISP objects
   
   
Heiko Kirschke

Focusing on application programming, persistency is an issue in many applications. There are different ways to achieve persistency, each having its benefits and drawbacks. This tutorial introduces an object-oriented approach at the example of Persistent LISP objects (aka Plob), an object-oriented, free available, self-contained database tailored specially for a seamless integration of persistency into Lisp and CLOS. Topics covered will be:

  • Database and Lisp concepts: Transactions, locks, schema evolution, reachability in databases, indexes, orthogonal and transparent persistency, identity.
  • Architecture and interna of Plob: Client/server approach, serverless Plob, internal data representation.
  • Database specific extensions introduced by Plob: Persistent packages, persistent symbols, persistent btrees.
  • Programming with Plob:
    • Application examples.
    • Different kinds of usage: Tight binding, loose binding, using the direct representation.
    • Persistency in legacy systems, top level declarations.
    • Performance issues.
    • Administration of Plob.
  • Installation issues.

Participants will learn how to use Plob for their own applications, and how to take maximum advantage of Plob.

Attendees should be familiar with Lisp. Further knowledge of CLOS and object-oriented or relational databases is helpful.

Heiko Kirschke is working as consultant at CSC Ploenzke, Hamburg. He has practiced computer science and object-oriented technology for over a decade as a designer, implementer, researcher and consultant.
